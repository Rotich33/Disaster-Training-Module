Emergency Kit Checklist

  • 3-day water supply (1 gallon/person/day)
  • Non-perishable food for 3 days
  • Battery-powered or hand-crank radio
  • Flashlight and extra batteries
  • First aid kit
  • Whistle to signal for help
  • Dust masks and plastic sheeting
  • Wrench or pliers to turn off utilities
  • Manual can opener
  • Local maps and cell phone charger
🚨

During a Disaster: DO's

  • Stay calm and follow your plan
  • Listen to official emergency broadcasts
  • Evacuate immediately if ordered
  • Stay away from floodwaters
  • Do NOT use candles (fire risk)
  • Check on elderly neighbors
  • Use text messages — calls may overload
  • Stay indoors if instructed (tornado/chemical)
  • Document damage for insurance
🏠

After a Disaster: Recovery Steps

  • Only return home when authorities say it's safe
  • Wear protective gear (gloves, boots)
  • Check for gas leaks before entering
  • Document all property damage
  • Avoid tap water until declared safe
  • Register with local disaster relief agency
  • Apply for FEMA assistance if eligible
  • Watch for signs of stress and seek help
🗺️

Family Emergency Plan

  • Designate an out-of-town contact person
  • Identify two evacuation routes from home
  • Choose two family meeting locations
  • Know your local emergency alert system
  • Keep copies of important documents
  • Plan for pets and special needs members
  • Practice the plan every 6 months
  • Know how to turn off home utilities
⚡

Types of Disasters to Know

  • 🌊 Floods — most common natural disaster
  • 🌀 Hurricanes — high winds + storm surge
  • 🌍 Earthquakes — Drop, Cover, Hold On
  • 🔥 Wildfires — evacuate early, don't wait
  • 🌪️ Tornadoes — seek interior room, lowest floor
  • ❄️ Winter Storms — stay indoors, conserve heat
  • ☣️ Hazmat Incidents — shelter-in-place
